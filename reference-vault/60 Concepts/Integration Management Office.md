## Definition

An Integration Management Office (IMO) is a temporary governance structure established to coordinate and track the workstreams of a post-merger integration. It typically includes a dedicated IMO lead (often from an external integration management firm), a master integration tracker, a weekly integration steerco, and defined escalation paths to the executive sponsor. The IMO is not a decision-making body — it is a coordination and visibility mechanism. Decision authority remains with the workstream owners and executive sponsors.

## Relevance to Project Atlas

[[Alpha Advisory]] ([[Sarah Torres]]) is running the Project Atlas IMO. The IMO owns the master integration tracker, the weekly steerco (Mondays), and the monthly executive steerco (with [[David Kim]] and [[Linda Park]]). The IMO structure was adopted at the Integration Kickoff (2026-01-20) and runs through Day 100 (2026-04-25). The IMO's value is cross-workstream dependency management — particularly the interaction between SSO Migration, Customer Comms, and Contract Novation, where timing dependencies are material.

## Counter-arguments

IMO structures add governance overhead and cost. [[David Kim]]'s concern about the $280K/month Alpha Advisory burn is a legitimate challenge — an IMO that runs longer than necessary or adds bureaucracy without commensurate value creates cost drag. The IMO is appropriate for the 100-day phase; post-Day 100, the coordination function should be absorbed internally.
