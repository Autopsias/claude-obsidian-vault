## Key facts

- BetaCo CTO and co-founder; built the entire data platform from scratch over 6 years
- Deep expertise in customer lifecycle analytics and event-stream architecture; the intellectual author of BetaCo's core product
- Cooperative in integration meetings but visibly protective of his engineering team — concerned about "assimilation" into Acme's larger org
- Has not been offered a defined role in the merged engineering structure; this is the central retention risk
- Reports dotted-line to [[Morgan Chen]] post-close; formally reports to [[Emma Walsh]] (SVP Product) on product decisions

## Role in Project Atlas

Primary BetaCo technical counterpart to [[Morgan Chen]]. Owns BetaCo-side delivery on SSO Migration (coordinating [[Tom Bradley]]) and is the key input on Data Residency architecture — his data platform design determines what can realistically stay in Azure UK South. Without his active cooperation, the Day 90 SSO target slips.

## Working style

Precise, evidence-driven, does not overstate confidence. Will say "I don't know yet" rather than guess. Pushes back when he thinks a timeline is unrealistic — usually correct when he does. Has a tendency to frame Acme decisions as "the Acme way" vs "the right way" which creates friction in cross-team meetings.
